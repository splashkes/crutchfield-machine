# Referenced Research Materials

Collected from the project chat **"Video feedback loops and GPU recreation"** (2026-04-19), "Who to read" section.

Seven entries. Three PDFs downloaded; the rest are books, web archives, or behind paywalls — canonical access links given.

---

## 1. Crutchfield — the video-feedback paper itself ✅

**James P. Crutchfield, "Space-Time Dynamics in Video Feedback." *Physica D: Nonlinear Phenomena* 10 (1984), 229–245.**

- File: `Crutchfield_1984_Vasulka.pdf` (19 pages, scanned via OmniPage)
- Source: https://vasulka.org/archive/Artists1/Crutchfield,Jim/Space-TimeDynamic.pdf
- DOI: 10.1016/0167-2789(84)90264-1

The one you start with. Proposes two models — a discrete iterated functional equation and a reaction-diffusion PDE — both supported by experimental photographs from a rig like yours.

## 2. Turing — reaction-diffusion origin ✅

**A. M. Turing, "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis." *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B* 237 (1952), 37–72.**

- File: `Turing_1952_ChemicalBasisOfMorphogenesis.pdf` (37 pages, MIT mirror)
- Source: https://www.mit.edu/~kardar/teaching/IITS/lectures/lec8/TuringPhilTransB1952.pdf
- DOI: 10.1098/rstb.1952.0012

The morphogen paper. Lens blur as diffusion, nonlinear response as reaction — why video feedback resembles Gray-Scott.

## 3. Kaneko — coupled map lattices ⚠️

**Kunihiko Kaneko, "Period-Doubling of Kink-Antikink Patterns, Quasiperiodicity in Antiferro-Like Structures and Spatial Intermittency in Coupled Logistic Lattice: Towards a Prelude of a 'Field Theory of Chaos.'" *Progress of Theoretical Physics* 72, no. 3 (1984), 480–486.**

- DOI: 10.1143/PTP.72.480
- Abstract & paywalled PDF: https://academic.oup.com/ptp/article/72/3/480/1906788
- Author's publications page: http://chaos.c.u-tokyo.ac.jp/~kaneko/papers_books.html

**Could not download** — Oxford Academic PTP and AIP mirrors (where his 1992 overview lives) both block automated access via Cloudflare. Accessible through a university library proxy or interlibrary loan. A good open-access companion that surveys the same ground is the *Scholarpedia* "Coupled maps" article (http://www.scholarpedia.org/article/Coupled_maps), co-authored by people in Kaneko's lineage. The definitive book, edited by Kaneko, is *Theory and Applications of Coupled Map Lattices* (Wiley, 1993).

## 4. Langton — edge of chaos ✅

**Chris G. Langton, "Computation at the Edge of Chaos: Phase Transitions and Emergent Computation." *Physica D* 42 (1990), 12–37.**

- File: `Langton_1990_ComputationEdgeOfChaos.pdf` (26 pages)
- Source: https://shinyverse.org/al4ai/papers/Langton.EdgeOfChaos.pdf
- DOI: 10.1016/0167-2789(90)90064-V

The λ-parameter paper — cellular automata poised between order and chaos maximize information transmission, storage, and modification. Directly applicable to your favorite feedback configurations.

## 5. Wolfram — *A New Kind of Science* 📖

**Stephen Wolfram, *A New Kind of Science.* Wolfram Media, 2002. 1,197 pages.**

- Full text free online: https://www.wolframscience.com/nks/
- Internet Archive backup: https://archive.org/details/newkindofscience00wolf

**Not downloaded** — it's a 1,200-page book and Wolfram makes the entire thing free to read page-by-page on his site. For the feedback discussion, the useful chapters are Chapter 6 ("Starting from Randomness," which covers Class 4 behavior and long transients directly) and Chapter 12 (computational irreducibility).

## 6. Kauffman — self-organization and basins of attraction 📖

**Stuart A. Kauffman, *The Origins of Order: Self-Organization and Selection in Evolution.* Oxford University Press, 1993. 709 pages.**

- Publisher page: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-origins-of-order-9780195079517
- ISBN 0-19-507951-5 (paperback)

**Not downloaded** — in-copyright book; access via library or purchase. The directly relevant parts are Part I ("Adaptation on the Edge of Chaos") on rugged fitness landscapes and Chapter 5 on self-organization in complex systems. If you want a shorter on-ramp first, his 1992 article "Origins of Order in Evolution: Self-Organization and Selection" (DOI 10.1007/978-94-015-8054-0_8) is the prolegomena to the book.

## 7. Steina & Woody Vasulka — the artist archive 🗂️

**The Vasulka Archive** — artist-documented video feedback experiments from the pioneers of the medium.

- Main archive: https://vasulka.org/
- Eigenwelt der Apparate-Welt PDF (where Crutchfield's paper is republished alongside artist writings): https://vasulka.org/Kitchen/PDF_Eigenwelt/pdf/191-207.pdf

Not a single paper — a corpus. Steina and Woody Vasulka spent decades doing exactly the kind of rig-building you describe, and wrote about it. The archive is where Crutchfield's paper found a home outside the physics literature, which tells you something about the cross-pollination.
